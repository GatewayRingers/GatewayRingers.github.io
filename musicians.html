Gateway Musicians

Chuck Peery, Director

Chuck Peery began directing the Gateway Ringers for the Spring, 2008 season. Chuck is the organist for Emmanuel Episcopal Church, Webster Groves. He taught public school choral music for 28 years in Ohio after having studied choral music education, organ, and composition at the College-Conservatory of Music of the University of Cincinnati. Chuck has handbell music published by the American Guild of English Handbell Ringers, and has written works on commission for churches in Ohio, Florida, Georgia and New York, as well as for the bell choir of the University of Southern Mississippi, and the Cincinnati Brass Band. In more recent years, Chuck has completed eight years of private study in harp with Ulrike Brinksmeier, chair of the harp department at the Cincinnati Conservatory.

  • * Carl Zimmerman is a charter member of The Gateway Ringers. He began ringing handbells more than 35 years ago, when the church he attended was given a two octave set - his expertise on tower bells led to his being drafted as the first director of a brand new handbell choir, so all learned together. Since then he has rung in or directed several other handbell choirs, especially at his church home for over 30 years - Webster Groves Presbyterian. He is a retired USAF weather officer, a retired computer systems analyst, the Webmaster for TowerBells.org, a tenor in the St.Louis Symphony Chorus and his church choir, and the expert to consult on the history and work of bellfounders in St.Louis. He and his wife are parents of two and grandparents of six.
